## 1) Product Purpose
The Francisco Ossian Portfolio (foln.dev) exists as a professional digital presence for a full-stack developer. Its primary purpose is to showcase technical skills, highlight completed projects, share knowledge through a blog, and facilitate direct contact with potential clients or collaborators. It solves the problem of professional visibility and provides a centralized hub for his professional identity.

## 2) Target Users
- **Potential Clients:** Looking for a full-stack developer for new projects, maintenance, or consultations.
- **Recruiters/Employers:** Evaluating technical skills and project experience.
- **Tech Community:** Reading blog posts and exploring shared projects.
- **Collaborators:** Interested in joint ventures or open-source activities.

## 3) Core User Flows
- **Exploring Professional Background:** Users land on the home page, read the "About" section and "What I do" to understand the developer's expertise.
- **Reviewing Projects:** Users scroll to the "Projects" section, interact with the project list to see details, and follow links to external repositories or live demos.
- **Consuming Technical Content:** Users navigate to the "Blog" section, browse post summaries, and read full articles.
- **Initiating Contact:** Users use the WhatsApp CTA buttons or social media links in the header/footer to start a conversation.

## 4) Core Features
- **Dynamic Project Showcase:** Interactive list of projects with categories, tags, and descriptive details.
- **Markdown-based Blog:** A reading experience for technical articles with support for syntax highlighting and mathematical formulas (LaTeX).
- **Service Highlights:** Explicit listing of offered services (Front-end, Back-end, Mobile, Cloud, Automations).
- **Integrated Contact Points:** Quick access to WhatsApp, LinkedIn, GitHub, and Email.
- **Responsive Animations:** Visual feedback and entry animations to enhance the "wow" factor.

## 5) Non-Goals
- **Direct E-commerce:** The site does not sell products or services directly through a checkout system.
- **Interactive User Community:** No comment sections, user accounts, or forums.
- **Content Management Interface:** The site itself does not provide a UI for editing content; it is purely a consumer of external data.
- **Real-time Chat:** No built-in chat widget; it redirects to external platforms (WhatsApp).

## 6) UX / Product Surface
- **Home Page:**
    - Hero section with main value proposition and primary CTA.
    - "About" Bento-style panel.
    - "What I do" highlight section.
    - Interactive "Projects" list.
- **Blog Listing Page:** Grid/List of available posts fetched from the CMS.
- **Blog Post Page:** Full-screen reading experience with markdown rendering.

## 7) Constraints (Product-Level)
- **Content Language:** Primarily in Portuguese (PT-BR).
- **Communication Channel:** Relies heavily on external platforms (WhatsApp, GitHub, LinkedIn) for interaction.
- **Update Frequency:** Dependent on the developer's manual updates to a separate GitHub repository.

## 8) Success Criteria
- **Professional Impression:** Quantified by the visual quality and smooth interactions (wow effect).
- **Contact Conversion:** Successful redirection of users to contact channels (primarily WhatsApp).
- **Information Accessibility:** Users being able to find project details and technical posts quickly.
